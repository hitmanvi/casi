                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                          WMS History

                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                          Williams Manufacturing Company was established back in 1943, and around 1974, it was rebranded to WMS Industries. At the same time, a new department was created to take over the company's business in developing pinball games based on innovative software and concepts. That was WMS's main field of operations until the 1990s, but it was abandoned after becoming unprofitable.

                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                          In 1991, WMS launched Williams Gaming and entered the state video lottery market. It was an endeavor with satisfactory success, which opened new horizons. In 1994, WMS decided to join the market of reel-spinning slot machines, and two years later, in 1996, the company introduced Reel 'em In. The brand was reused a decade later for the online slot Reel 'em In! Catch the Big One 2 and in 2015 for Reel 'em In! Big Bass Bucks.

                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                          The premiere was more than successful, and soon, the provider came out with Boom, Filthy Rich, and Jackpot Party, along with other multi-line slot cabinets. In 1999, Williams Gaming was incorporated and rebranded as WMS Gaming and continued with the development, sale, management, leasing, and licensing of gaming machines.

                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                          WMS suffered a major crisis in 2001, and the next several years after a glitch was found in its gaming software. Players were able to collect multiple credits without paying, and IGT, along with other studios, filed lawsuits against the provider. Anyway, the problem was fixed, and in 2003, WMS Gaming introduced the CPU-NXT video operating platform.

                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                          The Linux-based system quickly brought the brand among leaders and innovators of the industry. After more than a decade of successful projects, WMS Gaming attracted the interest of Scientific Games, a leading US game developer and marketer. In 2016, the corporation acquired all assets of WMS in a deal for $1.5 billion, despite the fact that it left Scientific Games with a debt of over $3 billion.

                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                          In March 2022, the gaming and online gambling divisions were separated from the company's lottery business and were rebranded to Light & Wonder. The WMS brand was retained, but no new slots have been released since then. However, a new WMS slot was announced for potential release in February 2025 - Kronos Megaways, so the final chapter of the studio's history may be upon us!
