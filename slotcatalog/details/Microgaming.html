                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                          Microgaming History

                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                          Not long ago, Microgaming was considered one of the main pillars of the iGaming industry.                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                           For reasons known to perhaps only a handful of people, in 2018, Microgaming rebranded to Apricot Investments.                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                           Let's rewind to the founding of the company that would soon change the way gambling was played. Martin Moshal founded Microgaming in 1994 with headquarters in Douglas, Isle of Man. It started with innovations right away and, for about 5 years, produced a number of software updates for slot cabinets.

                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                          But the more significant product was the first online casino ever - Gaming Club Online Casino.                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                           In 1998, Microgaming introduced Cash Splash - the first ever progressive jackpot slot, which remained hot for years before the release of Mega Moolah. For nearly 3 decades, Microgaming achieved a lot by shaping and reshaping the iGaming environment to make it as sophisticated as it is today, with the help of other leading developers, of course.

                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                          Now, back to present times, or at least almost! After the Apricot rebranding in 2016, major resources were relocated, and in 2020, the MPN, Microgaming Poker Network, was shut down. In 2022, the massive corporation Games Global acquired the Quickfire platform and most of Microgaming's slots and table games.

                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                          Games Global represents more than 900 global brands in different economic fields with a focus on entertainment.
