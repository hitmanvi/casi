                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                          IGT History

                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                          In 1975, William "Si" Redd, an American businessman, entered the video poker machine niche by registering A-1 Supply. The company had headquarters in Las Vegas, Nevada, and it was rebranded to Sircoma first in 1978 and IGT after that in 1981. During the same year, it went public for the first time.

                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                          International Game Technology took advantage of its vast success and used its revenues to expand rapidly in the next several decades. Besides manufacturing cutting-edge software and hardware for the gambling industry, IGT acquired multiple smaller businesses in the same niche.

                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                          Thus, it also acquired its patents on innovative systems and technology, like the CPT - Computerized Player Tracking. This concept was later used for the development of IGT's Advantage and SBX systems. In the late 1980s, IGT introduced the first million-dollar progressive jackpot slot machine - Nevada Megabucks.

                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                          IGT also founded a strategic office in Australia and got listed in NASDAQ. In 1991, the company was listed on the NYSE (New York Stock Exchange), and in 1992, new offices were founded across Europe and in South Africa and Argentina. In 1996, IGT got approval to start a slot series based on the TV show Wheel of Fortune.

                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                          In 1998, IGT made a new brilliant strategic move by acquiring Barcrest and the major Native American market's distributor Sodak Gaming. This ensured exclusive access to certain audiences, technology patents, and distribution networks. Barcrest was sold to Light & Wonder in 2010.

                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                          In 2001, IGT became part of the S&P 500 index and continued to obtain licenses and certificates across the globe. The company also continued its policy to buy smaller companies with unique software patents. After more than a decade of success around the world, IGT and the lottery giant GTEC merged in 2015.

                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                          The Italian lottery company, also popular for its dedication to manufacturing gaming equipment, paid $6.4 billion to guarantee the deal. The new holding company was named International Game Technology PLC (IGT) with headquarters in the UK and operational centers in Rome, Las Vegas, and Providence, Rhode Island.
